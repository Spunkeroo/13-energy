How to Switch Providers

  1. Find your current rate on your electricity bill (look for "supply charge" or "energy charge" per kWh)
  2. Enter your ZIP code on a comparison site to see available plans
  3. Compare fixed-rate plans at different term lengths (12, 24, 36 months)
  4. Switch online in 5 minutes — your utility still delivers the power, you just pay less for the supply
  5. There's no service interruption when switching

How to Lower Your Electric Bill (Any State)

  • Smart thermostat: Saves $131-$145/year (recommended: Ecobee, Nest, Honeywell)
  • LED bulbs: Saves $225/year for an average home
  • Weatherstripping: $20-50 in materials saves $100-200/year on heating/cooling
  • Energy audit: Many utilities offer free home energy audits that identify your biggest savings opportunities
  • Solar panels: Eliminates 80-100% of your electric bill with 6-8 year payback
  • Time-of-use plans: Shift energy use to off-peak hours for 30-50% lower rates

Why Electricity Rates Keep Rising

  • Grid infrastructure: Aging power lines and transformers require billions in upgrades
  • Renewable transition: Building new solar and wind capacity requires upfront investment
  • Electrification: EVs, heat pumps, and data centers are increasing demand
  • Extreme weather: More frequent storms and heatwaves strain the grid and increase costs
  • Inflation: Labor, materials, and fuel costs pass through to ratepayers

The average US residential electricity rate has increased 4.2% annually since 2020. At this pace, rates will be 25% higher by 2030 — making energy-saving investments increasingly valuable.

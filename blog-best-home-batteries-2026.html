1. Tesla Powerwall 3 — Best Overall

13.5 kWh11.5 kW output$12,500 installed10-year warranty

The Powerwall 3 is Tesla's most powerful home battery yet. The integrated solar inverter simplifies installation and reduces costs. 11.5 kW of continuous power means it can run your entire home — including AC, EV charging, and kitchen appliances — during an outage.

Best features: Highest continuous power output of any single residential battery. Integrated inverter eliminates need for separate hardware. Tesla app provides real-time monitoring and Storm Watch auto-charging before weather events.

The Powerwall 3 is the best home battery for most homeowners. It offers the highest power output, a competitive price, and Tesla's excellent app experience. The integrated inverter makes installation simpler and cheaper.
9.1/10

2. Enphase IQ Battery 5P — Most Flexible

5 kWh per unit3.84 kW per unit$6,000/unit15-year warranty

Enphase's modular approach lets you start small and add capacity over time. Each 5 kWh unit works independently, so there's no single point of failure. Stack up to 4 units (20 kWh, 15.36 kW) for whole-home backup.

Best features: Industry-leading 15-year warranty. Modular design — start with one and add more later. No single point of failure. Works with any solar system (not just Enphase microinverters).

Best for homeowners who want flexibility and the longest warranty in the industry. Start with one unit for essential loads, add more as budget allows. Slightly lower power per dollar than Tesla, but unmatched reliability.
9.0/10

3. Franklin WH aPower2 — Best Value

15 kWh10 kW output$12,000 installed12-year warranty

Franklin Whole Home is the newcomer that's disrupting the market. At $12,000 for 15 kWh and 10 kW, it's the best value per kWh of any premium battery. The 12-year warranty splits the difference between Tesla (10) and Enphase (15).

If budget matters, the aPower2 offers the most capacity and power for the lowest price. It's 20% cheaper per kWh than Tesla and nearly matches it on power output. A serious contender.

How to Choose the Right Home Battery

For Backup Power Only

If your main goal is keeping lights, fridge, and WiFi on during outages: Enphase IQ 5P (1-2 units). Affordable entry point at $6,000-$12,000, 15-year warranty, works with any solar system.

For Whole-Home Backup

If you want to run AC, kitchen, and EV charger during outages: Tesla Powerwall 3. The 11.5 kW output handles high-draw appliances that other batteries can't.

For Maximum Savings

If you're on time-of-use rates and want to maximize bill savings: Franklin WH aPower2. 15 kWh stores enough solar energy to avoid peak rates entirely, and the low price means faster payback.

For Future Expansion

Enphase IQ 5P is the most scalable. Add 5 kWh units as your needs grow — perfect if you plan to add an EV, heat pump, or pool.

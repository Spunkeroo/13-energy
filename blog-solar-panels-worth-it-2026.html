Are Solar Panels Worth It in 2026? Complete Cost-Benefit Analysis

Updated March 1, 2026 · 14 min read · by 13.Energy

The short answer: yes, for most homeowners, solar panels are absolutely worth it in 2026. The 30% federal tax credit is still available, panel prices have dropped 70% in the last decade, and electricity rates continue rising 3-5% annually. Here's the complete breakdown with real numbers.

The Bottom Line

A typical 8kW solar system costs $22,800 before incentives, $15,960 after the 30% federal tax credit. It saves $1,800-$2,400/year on electricity, pays for itself in 6-8 years, and generates $45,000-$60,000 in savings over 25 years. Solar panels also increase home value by $15,000-$25,000.

The 30% Federal Tax Credit (ITC)

The Investment Tax Credit (ITC) lets you deduct 30% of your total solar installation cost from your federal income taxes. This is a dollar-for-dollar tax credit, not a deduction — it directly reduces your tax bill.

ITC Timeline

  • 2022-2032: 30% tax credit (current rate)
  • 2033: Steps down to 26%
  • 2034: Steps down to 22%
  • 2035+: Expires for residential (unless renewed)

Act before 2033 to lock in the full 30% credit. On a $22,800 system, that's $6,840 in tax savings.

When Solar Panels Are NOT Worth It

Solar isn't right for everyone. Here's when you should hold off:

  • Heavy shade: If your roof gets less than 4 hours of direct sun daily, production will be too low for a good ROI
  • Old roof: If your roof needs replacement within 5 years, do the roof first. Removing and reinstalling panels costs $1,500-$3,000
  • Moving soon: If you're selling within 2-3 years, you may not recoup costs despite the home value increase
  • Very cheap electricity: If you pay less than $0.10/kWh (some parts of Washington, Idaho), the payback period stretches beyond 12 years
  • HOA restrictions: Some HOAs still restrict solar installations, though most states now have solar access laws

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do solar panels last?

Modern solar panels last 25-30 years with minimal degradation. Most manufacturers guarantee at least 80% production after 25 years. Inverters typically last 10-15 years and may need one replacement.

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes, but at reduced efficiency — about 25-40% of peak production. Panels work with diffuse light, not just direct sunlight. Germany gets less sun than most US states and is one of the world's solar leaders.

Should I lease or buy solar panels?

Buying is almost always better financially. You get the full tax credit, you own the asset, and your ROI is 2-3x higher than leasing. Leasing makes the installer rich, not you. If you can't afford to buy, a solar loan at 5-7% is still better than leasing.

What about net metering?

Net metering lets you sell excess solar energy back to the grid. Policies vary by state — some offer full retail rate credits (best for solar owners), while others offer wholesale rates. Check your state's net metering policy before going solar.

Will solar panels damage my roof?

Properly installed solar panels actually protect the portion of roof they cover from weather. Reputable installers use flashing and sealant that maintain roof integrity. Always use licensed, insured installers with strong reviews.
